General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Crack sealing and filling are preventive maintenance treatment methods, which are used to reduce and/or prevent water penetration along pavement surface cracks (e.g., longitudinal, transverse, and diagonal cracks) into sub-layers.   The pavement in general needs to be in good/fair condition, i.e., no alligator cracking (in GDOT’s COPACES, it is load cracking severity level 3 or 4). Figure below shows a good and poor candidate for crack sealing. These techniques are considered effective when applied to cracks less than 1 inch in width, and should not be considered if the pavement is structurally defective, e.g., pavement with alligator cracking (load cracking severity level 3 or 4) and/or excessive rutting, or if the pavement is scheduled to be resurfaced or replaced.   Crack sealing/filling does not add structural capacity.
