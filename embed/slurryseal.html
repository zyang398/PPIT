General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Slurry seal is the application of “a mixture of asphalt emulsion, graded aggregates, mineral filler and water in a free flowing consistency.” (NCPP).   Where chip seals are similar in composition, slurry seals use finer aggregate (<4.75 mm /passing #4 sieve) to allow the mixture to flow and be spread as a thin wearing surface.  Material used as mineral filler may include Portland cement, lime or fly ash.  The mixture is placed continuously over an existing pavement.  The treatment seals the pavement, restores surface texture and can reduce continued raveling of the surface. Slurry seals are defined as Type I, II and III based on the range of the aggregate gradation, with Type I the finest and Type III the coarsest gradation.   The type is related to the severity of raveling and the traffic level (FHWA pocket Guide).   GDOT’s aggregate gradation in standard specification Section 427 most closely matches Type II.   The mixture should be designed based on the specific materials to be used.
