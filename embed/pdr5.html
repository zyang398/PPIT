General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Using pavement life as a criteria, usually PDR can provide 5 to 15 years of service before further repairs are needed(Irene K. Battaglia, 2012). Early studies of PDR showed that 80 to 100 percent of properly installed repairs should perform well after 3 to 10 years. However, failure in as little as 2 or 3 years is possible if PDR is constructed with unsound materials or poor workmanship. (Smith, Hoerner, & Peshkin, 2008; T.P. Wilson, 1999)

The most frequent causes of early PDR failure include(Irene K. Battaglia, 2012; T.P. Wilson, 1999):

• Improper repair materials

• Inadequate bond between the patch and existing pavement

• Insufficient consolidation

• Compression failure, which means the crushing of a repair due to expansion of the surrounding pavement during a freeze-thaw cycle.

• Incompatible thermal expansion between the patch and existing pavement

• Feathering of the patch material which means the thin placement of patch materials because of shallow patch edges that do not allow adequate depth of placement

But due to a lot of reasons such as condition of existing concrete, the extent of the deterioration, and future traffic loads, the life can be very different and difficult to compare.
