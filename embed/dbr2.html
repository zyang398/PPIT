General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Dowel Bar Retrofit is suitable for pavements that are structurally sound but shows low load transfer at joints. It is stated by Caltrans that pavements 1) little remaining life (extensive cracking (more than 10% stage 3 cracking)) 2) high severity joint defects are not suitable candidates.

1. Suitable Candidates

1) Structural condition of slabs should be good.

Caltrans: D-cracking, alkali-silica reaction (ASR) or alkali-carbonate reaction (ACR) distress, multiple transverse cracking, or significant longitudinal cracking are poor candidates for DBR (Maintenance 2008).

WSDOT: Dowel-bar retrofit is appropriate on PCC pavements with <10% slab=slab replacement=replacement

2) Structural condition of base should be good.

Caltrans: Slabs with a high deflection value at the joints are generally an indication of poor base condition (Maintenance 2008).

3) Load transfer efficiency.

Caltrans: LTE < 60 are good candidates. LTE is tested using the FWD, shown in Figure.

LTE testing using FWD (Pierce, Uhlmeyer et al. 2003)

4) Faulting value

Caltrans: Pavements with faulting > 0.10 in. (2 1⁄2 mm) but < 0.5 in. (13 mm are good candidates. Faulting < 0.10 in. (2 1⁄2 mm) does not warrant DBR. If faulting is greater than 0.5 in. (13 mm) and/or if the pavement is structurally inadequate, reconstruction should be considered (Pierce et al, 2003)

WSDOT: Dowel-bar retrofit is appropriate on PCC pavements average faulting of 3 to 13 mm (1⁄8 to 1⁄2 in.).

5) Joints or cracks condition.

Caltrans: Joints or transverse cracks should exhibit low to moderate severity spalling, or better (Maintenance 2008).
