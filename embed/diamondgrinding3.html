Diamond grinding equipment uses diamond saw blades that are gang mounted on a cutting head. The three most factors which are to be considered for a grinding machine are: 1) weight of the machine; 2) the horsepower available to the grinding head, and 3) the grinding head (Correa and Wong 2001).

 

 

Grinding Equipment

 

Diamond Saw Blade and cutting head (Caltrans DOT, 2008)

The front wheels pass over a bump or fault, and the centrally mounted cutting head will shaved off the bump or fault. The rear wheels follow in the smooth path left by the grinding head. The cutting head has a width ranging from 910 to 970 mm (36 to 38 inches) usually while newer machines may goes up to 1200 mm (47 inches). Generally, appropriate texture is produced by machine with spacing of 164 to 194 diamond blades per meter (50 to 60 per foot) of shaft (Correa and Wong 2001).
