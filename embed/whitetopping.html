Whitetopping allows for an alternative to asphalt overlays for existing asphalt pavements, especially where recurring rutting or shoving has been demonstrated or expected due to truck loadings (either experienced or expected). Whitetopping in Georgia has commonly been associated with intersection areas that have recurrent or excessive rutting due to stopping and turning movements from heavy trucks, but the method can be used for any length of pavement. The word whitetopping itself has been replaced in recent literature with the terms unbonded concrete overlay over asphalt (UCOA) or bonded concrete overlay over asphalt (BCOA). UCOA was known as conventional whitetopping and typically is now basically designed like a new pavement on a stable base, so the thickness can range from 4 inches to 11 inches. BCOA was once termed ultra-thin whitetopping and it typically is 2 to 6 inches in thickness.
