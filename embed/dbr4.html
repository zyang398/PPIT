The procedure of dowel bar retrofit includes cutting slots, remove concrete from slots, clean slots, place silicone at all joints/cracks, place dowel bar assemblies in slots, place patching material, consolidate patching material, finish patching material, and diamond grinding. The filed operation of dowel bar retrofit can be categorized into 1) slots preparation, 2) dowel bars preparation, 3) dowel bars placement, 4) patching, and 5) diamond grinding.

1) Preparing slots

Dowel Bar Retrofit equipment uses diamond tipped saw blade to cut pavements. Figure below shows the blade and the concrete surface after slot cutting.

The saw cuts were approximately 5 1⁄2 inches deep (Bischoff and Toepel 2002). However, the depth of saw cutting should be determined carefully by the contractor. Usually the contractor should measure the depth by placing a dowel bar in the dowel bar chairs, the depth is equal to half of the depth from the dowel to the bottom of the chairs and one half the pavement depth. Sometimes this measurement varies from what is specified.

Slots: 1⁄2 inches wide and were spaced 12 inches apart on the center(Pierce, Uhlmeyer et al. 2003). And slots should be aligned to miss existing longitudinal cracks.

Cutting Slots(Bischoff and Toepel 2002)

After cutting slots, a jackhammer is used to clear the slots. A jackhammer can’t be used vertically to the plane of the pavement because it will punch through the floor of the slots. Many contractors use 15-pound jackhammer.

After jackhammering, workers scraped the concrete debris from the slots with pickaxes.

Next, the slots were sandblasted and blowed using compresses air. Concrete chunks, dirt, debris, water, and slurry should be all cleaned after these steps. During the cleaning process, the public should be protected from sand and blasting debris.

Compressed air is used to blow any debris out of the slots (Bischoff and Toepel 2002)

2) Dowel bar preparation

There are mainly two types of dowel bars: 1) Epoxy coated steel dowel bars and 2) Nuovinox stainless steel clad dowel bars.

For example, the Wisconsin DOT use the following 2 types of dowel bars for one of their studies: 1) The standard epoxy coated steel dowel bars, which were 18 inches long and 1 1⁄4-inch diameter; 2) The Nuovinox dowel bars, manufactured by Stelax Industries, Ltd., which were 15 inches long with a 1 1⁄4-inch diameter. After preparation, each dowel bar is fitted with two end caps, two chairs, and a foam board (Bischoff and Toepel 2002). Once the slots had been cleaned, dowel bars had been prepared, then the dowel bars are ready to be put into the slots. Contractors should make sure that the dowel bar placement has the correct position. Figure below shows the dowel bars that have been placed and aligned in slots over a transverse crack. Generally, 3 dowel bars are placed per wheelpath because of the study done by Florida, which showed that three dowel bars performed as well as 5 dowel bars for faulting. Tayabji (1986) identified the following categories of dowel misalignment: Horizontal translation, Longitudinal translation (side shift), Vertical translation (depth error), Horizontal skew (horizontal misalignment), Vertical tilt (vertical misalignment).

3) Patching

The slots are blown clean and wet down with water before the patching material is poured in the slots. And then the pathing material was run with mobile mixer until it reached the desired consistency. Then the material is backfilling into the slots and consolidated by a spud vibrator around the dowel bar. Figure below shows that a spud vibrator is inserted in the slot on each side of the foam board to consolidate the patch material around the dowel bar.

4) Diamond grinding

Diamond grinding should be within 10 days after the dowel bar placement in order to prevent road users' concern about the surface roughness. Diamond grinding can restore the road surface smoothness. After diamond grinding, remove the grinding residue from the roadway surface immediately.

Issues:

During the dowel bar retrofit process, there might exist many issues, and these issues can be divided into 3 aspects: pavement issues, patch material issues, dowel bar issues.

Pavement surface may have some issues such as thin pavement, bottom up deterioration and wearing slabs. (Bischoff and Toepel 2002)
