General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Chip seal is a pavement preservation treatment that addresses pavement distresses such as raveling, flushing, minor cracks, and provides a new skid resistant wearing surface to aged pavements.  Advantages of chip seals include improved skid resistance, rapidity and ease of construction, and good durability that help extend service life of pavements (NCPP).

Chip seals are not designed for enhancing structural capacity of pavements, instead, they are a means of preventive maintenance that can effectively enhance pavement surface condition and extend pavement service life when applied to pavements with limited or no distress (Gransberg and James 2005; Peshkin et al. 2004; Shuler et al. 2011).   Pavements with severe distress or pavements that are extensively deteriorated, on the other hand, are not suitable for chip seal application.   Chip seals cannot bridge cracks wider than 1/4 inch or properly address ruts greater than 3/8 inch in depth. Chip seals can produce a rough, open texture that may produce high noise and they may not be as smooth as asphalt mixtures.  Stones may be dislodged at early stages of service, causing a concern of broken windshields if not properly constructed. Modified binders and using sand seals with the chip seal can alleviate some of these concerns.
