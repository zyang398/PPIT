General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Slurry Seal

Seal any hairline cracks, but ensure that the sealant is not excessive. Mill any elevated areas.   Apply a tack coat immediately prior to the slurry seal.  Uniformly spread the slurry seal over the pavement in a continuous manner.

Weather Considerations

It is recommended that slurry seal be conducted when the temperature is warm.   The standard specification Section 427 provides seasonal limitations on the placement of slurry seal.   The acceptable dates generally range from April to October, with the tightest time frames for the northern zones, and the widest range (April 1 to October 31st ) allowed for the southern zones.  Do not proceed if there is moisture on pavement surface or in cracks, or if rain is imminent.

Slurry Seal Operation (source: Bill Evans, Ergon)

Traffic Considerations

Proper traffic control plans should be developed and maintained before and during the slurry seal operations.   Construct the work zone according to GDOT requirements.  The pavement should not be opened to traffic until the slurry seal has cured enough to withstand marring and tearing, and no water will be pumped to the surface (GDOT Specification Section 427).

Surface Preparation

Pavement surface should be dry and clean.   Any cracks wider than 1/8 in (3 mm) should be sealed.

Equipment Inspection

The machine to place the slurry seal should be checked for calibration, relatively clean and not containing worn parts.

Slurry Seal Application

Follow the specifications for installation temperatures.   Apply a tack coat to the pavement prior to the slurry seal.   Pre-wet the surface by fogging, if necessary.   Verify the application rate and consistency of the slurry seal at the start and periodically during application.

Weather Considerations

It is recommended that microsurfacing be conducted when the temperature is warm.   The standard specification Section 428 requires a minimum ambient and pavement temperature.    Do not proceed if there is moisture on pavement surface or in cracks, or if rain or freezing temperatures are imminent.

Microsurfacing Operation (source: NCPP )

Traffic Considerations

Proper traffic control plans should be developed and maintained before and during the microsurfacing operations.   Construct the work zone according to GDOT requirements.   The pavement should not be opened to traffic until the microsurfacing has cured enough to withstand marring and tearing (GDOT Specification Section 428).

Surface Preparation

Pavement surface should be dry and clean.  Any cracks wider than 1/8 in (3 mm) should be sealed.   Existing ruts should be filled with a leveling course.  A rut box must be used for any ruts over 1/2 inches.   Ruts should be filled as noted in Figure below.

Microsurfacing Application

Follow the specifications for installation temperatures.   Apply a tack coat to the pavement prior to application of the microsurfacing.    Pre-wet the surface by fogging, if necessary.   Verify the application rate and consistency at the start and periodically during application.  The mixture should be placed with a mechanical-type spreader box equipped with paddles or other devices to agitate and spread the material uniformly.
