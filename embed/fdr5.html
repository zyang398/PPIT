General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Appropriateness of FDR's use, the quality of construction, and the properties of the repair material all together determine the performance of FDR.

Full-depth repair may perform well but not be cost-effective for a pavement which has little or no remaining structural life and will soon need resurfacing or reconstruction.

Concrete pavement restoration is estimated to have a service life in the range of 5 to 15 years, depending on the combination of techniques used, and the extent, materials, and construction quality of each (NCHRP). The American Concrete Pavement Association estimates a service life of 10 to 15 years for full-depth repair on its own.
