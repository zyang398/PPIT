Hot-Applied Thermoplastic Sealant Materials

Thermoplastic sealants are bitumen-based materials that typically soften upon heating and harden upon cooling, usually without a change in chemical composition. These sealants have different elastic and thermal properties. They are also affected by weathering to some degree. Thermoplastic sealants are typically applied in a heated form on concrete pavements.

Polymer-modified/ground tire rubber-modified asphalt is the sealing industry standard. Different types and amounts of polymers and melted rubber are mixed into asphalt cement. The resulting sealants possess a large working range with respect to low-temperature extensibility and resistance to high-temperature softening and tracking. In recent years, softer grades of asphalt cement have been used in polymerized/rubberized asphalts to further improve low temperature extensibility. Cold-Applied Thermosetting Sealant Materials

Thermosetting sealants are typically one or two component materials that either set by the release of solvents or cure through a chemical reaction. Some of these sealants have shown potential for good performance, but the material costs are generally higher than standard polymerized/rubberized asphalt. However, thermosetting sealants are often placed thinner and have slightly lower labor and equipment costs because it requires less time for daily preparation and cleanup. It has no initial material heating and no purging of lines and pump.

A variety of thermosetting sealant materials is available, including polysulfides, polyurethanes, and silicones. Among these, silicones have been most widely used in pavement applications and have demonstrated long-term performance capabilities. Polysulfide and polyurethane sealants are not widely used in highway sealing and resealing operations.

Silicone sealants are one-part cold-applied materials that exhibit good extensibility and strong resistance to weathering. These sealants have good bonding strength in combination with a low modulus, so they are able to be placed thinner than the thermoplastic sealants.
