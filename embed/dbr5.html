The performance of dowel bar retrofit can be expressed in life extension, and it is generally believed that dbr can extend current pavement life by 10 to 15 years. There are a lot of study on dbr performance. Georgia and Puerto Rico started their DBR projects in the 1980’s (FHWA, 1991 & Gulden and Brown, 1987). Washington State completed their first DBR project in 1992 and that state has over 10 years of performance data for such projects (Pierce et al, 2003). California also started DBR projects in the 1990s and have extensive performance data.

Dowel Bar Retrofit together diamond grinding in most cases is a cost-effective method for PCC treatment for concrete pavement that have the faulting and bumping distress but structurally sound. The performance of this treatment method relies on appropriate project selection, dowel bar design features, patching materials and construction quality. And it is proven by many agencies that dbr with diamond grinding can extend the concrete service life, improve ride ability, and improve on load transfer efficiency.
