General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Micromilling & thin overlay is used to replace only the top, worn-out porous asphalt layer (e.g., OGFC and PEM) while the underlying layer (e.g., SMA) is in good condition. The rule of thumb for selecting micromilling & thin overlay project is the underlying layers must be structurally sound. The following are guidelines for the selection of micro-milling projects:

• The porous layer (e.g., OGFC and PED) wears out. For example, extensive raveling on the surface layer.

• Three factors are used to check for micromilling suitability: core, rutting, and Hamburg test.

• Rutting: limited rutting (less than 1⁄4"). Rutting greater than 1⁄4" is a concern.

• Core: measure the deformation of each layer to identify the underneath pavement structure

• Hamburg test: 12.5 mm is the failure threshold.
