General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Hot-mix

The types of HMA applied can be dense graded, open graded, stone matrix mixes, with or without high percentage of polymer-modified binder. This section focuses on dense graded layer and do not address other mix types, such as open-graded mixes. These materials and their construction requirements are described in Sections 400, 424, 824, and 828 of the Standard Specifications, 2013 Edition, in Special Provisions 400 and 828 issued Feb. 24, 2006, and in Special Provisions 424 and 824 issued Mar. 6, 2006.

According to GDOT's Asphalt Pavement Selection Guidelines, mix types are distinguished according to nominal maximum size of the aggregate in millimeters, i.e. 9.5 relates to an aggregate size of 9.5 millimeters, or 3/8 inch. Cold-mix

Cold-mixes for patching use the same designation as regular asphaltic concrete mixtures (i.e. 9.5 mm), but there may be slightly different requirements, therefore refer to the cold mix specification 401 for the required composition of cold-mix types.

Mastic Patching

Requirements for Mastic patching materials are found in Specification 827. Insure that the materials used provide a well bonded, flexible, durable and traffic resistant repair when properly applied.

Spray Injection

Emulsion: When the surface temperature is more than 50 °F, use R rapid set CRS-2 OR HFE150. When the surface temperature is less than 50 °F, use M medium set CMS-1 or 2.

Aggregates: Standard state spec. rock for chip sealing has common size ¼ 10, 3/8”, ½”, 5/8”. For ride ability and making tight mix, use smaller size except the shoulders.
