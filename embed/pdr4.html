General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Weather Considerations

Review manufacturers’ installation instructions for requirements specific to the patch material being used. Ensure that air and surface temperature meet manufacturer and contract requirements (typically 4 °C [40 °F] and rising) for concrete placement. Ensure that patching does not proceed if rain is imminent.

Traffic Considerations

Signs, devices, and all the set up are properly implemented, including trained personnel. The repaired pavement is not opened to traffic until the patch material meets strength requirements. Signs, devices are removed when preservation finishes.

Construction Steps

1) Determine the repair boundaries

The repair area is first defined by listening to the sound given by a chain, ball peen hammer or steel pipe hitting the deteriorated pavement. Then the boundaries for sawing and milling will be marked based on this. It is noted that the boundaries should extent 2 to 4 in. beyond the visible distressed area, and suggest value for PDR are as the following: Length: 15 in; Width: 10 in; Depth: 2 in.(Daniel P. Frentress, 2011).

2) Concrete Removal

Concrete removal is often achieved by two methods: Saw Cutting and milling, among which the saw cutting method is the most common one. However, It is recommended that patches be prepared using milling rather than saw cutting since saw cutting requires more time and patching material to fill repair areas (FHWA, 1999; Ronald Mulvaney, 2014). Different kinds of removal methods can refer to the report by Frentress & Harrington in 2012.
