General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Chip seal is the application of a thin layer of bituminous binder immediately followed by the application of a layer of crushed gravel (chips), which is compacted and embedded into the binder using a pneumatic (rubber tired) roller.   Chip seal is also called Bituminous surface treatment or BST.  Chip seals are typically more economical than regular asphalt mixtures.  They can protect the subgrade by preventing water from penetrating through cracks.   They can restore skid-resistance to worn or polished pavement.  They have also been used as a crack-relief interlayer along with an asphalt mixture resurfacing to reduce reflective cracking.   A variety of chip seal types can be used:

1)     Single Chip Seal:

A single-course chip seal is the most common type of chip seals.   It consists of a single layer of bituminous binder followed by a single layer of aggregate one particle thick, as shown in the following figure.

Single Chip Seal (NCHRP 2005)

2)     Double/Multiple Chip Seal:

A double or multiple chip seal is a seal coating consisting of multiple applications of bituminous binder and aggregate.   As shown in the following figure, a double chip seal consists of a layer of bituminous binder, a layer of uniform graded aggregate, then another layer of binder, and another layer of smaller aggregate.   The second and subsequent layers of aggregates is approximately one-half the size of the stone used in the previous layers.

Double Chip Seal (Gransberg and James 2005)

3)     Single Chip Seal with Choke Stone/Sand Seal (Also called Racked-in Seal):

This type of chip seal consists of a single chip seal covered with crushed fine aggregate to the surface that is applied to be locked in the voids of the seal.  The choke stone provides interlock between the binder and the seal, which then prevents the loss of aggregate before binder becomes fully cured.

Racked-in Seal (Gransberg and James 2005)

Scrub seal is an application that is very close to a chip seal treatment where asphalt emulsion and crushed rock are placed on an asphalt pavement surface. The only difference is that the asphalt emulsion is applied to the road surface through a series of brooms placed at different angles.
