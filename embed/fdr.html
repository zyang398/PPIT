Full Depth Repair is one method of Concrete Pavement Restoration (CPR) program which is widely known and used to almost all transpotation agencies. It is defined as the cast-in-place concrete repairs that extend the full-depth of the existing slab. Its purposes are to repair localized distress and to make the preparation for an overlay, as necessary, resulting in resorted ride ability, structural integrity and preventing further deterioration.

According to California DOT, full depth repair is suitable for transverse cracking with medium and high severity level, longitudinal cracking with medium and high severity level, corner breaks with low, medium and high severity levels, spalling with medium and high severity level, blowup with low, medium and high severity level, D-Cracking with medium and high severity level, and deterioration of existing repairs.
