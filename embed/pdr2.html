General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

The distress of concrete is either structural or functional. Typical structural distress includes cracking and joint deterioration, while typical functional di stress includes loading, long joint-spacing, shallow or late joint sawing, base or edge restraint and joint lock-up(ACPA, 1997 ).

Suitable candidate for PDR:

• Concrete Pavement with Spalling distress which is caused by the intrusion of incompressible materials into the joints and cracks, by poor consolidation or inadequate curing, by localized areas of scaling, weak concrete, clay balls, or high steel.

• Concrete Pavement with Surface scaling or deterioration in the top third of the slab caused by an inadequate air void system. (FHWA, 2015; Frentress & Harrington, 2012; Irene K. Battaglia, 2012)

Not Suitable candidates for PDR:

• Concrete Pavement with spalling of cracks caused by shrinkage, fatigue or foundation movement.

• The cases either suitable or not suitable by different authors.

• Concrete Pavement with spalling caused by dowel bar misalignment or lockup.

• Spalling caused by D‐cracking or reactive aggregate is recommend by some agencies while not by others(Irene K. Battaglia, 2012).
