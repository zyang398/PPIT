General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Weather and proper methods are critical for chip seal performance.

Weather/Environment Considerations

It is required that chip seals be constructed when the temperature is warm.     The standard specification Section 424 limits construction to between April 15 and October 15 and also requires a minimum ambient and pavement temperature.  Do not proceed if there is moisture on the pavement surface, or if rain or freezing temperatures are imminent.   The relative humidity can affect the amount of time for an asphalt emulsion to cure, therefore the specification also has a caution when the temperature is warm but the relative humidity is high.  In addition, high temperatures may also lead to lower viscosity in the binder, causing aggregate being picked up by the rubber tires of the rollers.

Traffic Considerations

Proper traffic control plans should be developed and maintained before and during the chip seal operations.  Construct the work zone according to GDOT requirements.   The pavement should not be opened to traffic until the bituminous material has cured sufficiently to ensure that the aggregate will not be loosened, dislodged, or whipped off by slow moving traffic (GDOT Specification Section 424).

Low-speed traffic may be allowed to travel on the fresh seal after the sweep, a pilot vehicle should be used to control the speed.   Traffic will continue to compact the seal to its full embedment along with time.  

Surface Preparation

a. Before applying chip sealing, the existing pavement surface should be structurally sound.

b. Patching and/or crack sealing may be needed if alligator cracking, longitudinal/transverse cracking are present.

c. Prime the surface (spray a light application of asphalt emulsion diluted 50:50 with water on the surface so), if required, so that the actual binder for the chip seal will not be absorbed into the surface and cause loss of aggregate after opened to traffic.

d. Clean the pavement surface with a power broom, power blower, hand brooms, or other means such that it is free of rocks, dust, and debris.

Binder (Emulsion) Application

a. The binder distributor should be calibrated before spraying. Check that it has a properly functioning volume level gauge, a sample valve and a hand hose.

b. The spray pattern should be checked for uniformity and proper double overlap.

c. Always start and stop the binder application by spraying on top of heavy paper that helps create a clear transverse joint between the chip seal and the existing pavement

Aggregate (Chip) Application

1)     Apply chips immediately, before the fresh emulsion sets

2)     Minimize truck movements on the fresh emulsion

3)     The aggregate spreader should have a scalping screen to remove any large particles. Check the aggregate spreaders calibration and check for wear.

Scrub Seal Application

Rolling

a.      Rubber tire (pneumatic) rollers provide the initial compaction of the aggregate.   A steel wheeled roller may also be used if it does not fracture the aggregate, but a minimum of two rubber tired rollers are preferred.

b.      The speed of rolling is a key consideration.  In hot weather, rolling should be expedited to ensure proper embedment of aggregate.   However, if rollers travel too fast, proper embedment will not be achieved.

Rubber-tired rollers

Brooming

a.      Broom the new surface to remove any excess chips as soon as after rolling and before it is opened to traffic

b.      During a multiple chip seal operation, sweeping is needed between the application of courses

c.      Take care not to unseat bonded stone when sweeping

Power Broom

Opening to Traffic

Do not allow traffic on an individual course until the bituminous material has cooled or set enough to ensure that the aggregates will not be loosened, dislodged, or whipped off by slow-moving traffic.
