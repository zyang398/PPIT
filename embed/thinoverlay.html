General Project Selection Material Construction Performance Standards/References

Thin overlay is the placement of an asphalt layer on top of existing or milled pavement surface. Defined by National Asphalt Pavement Association, thin overlay is “a time-proven method of extending the life of pavement structures that are still in serviceable shape, which is 1.5inches or less in thickness, and comprised of aggregates that have a small nominal maximum aggregate size, generally 12.5mm or less”. Thin overlay is often carried out in conjunction with other corrective activities such as milling and leveling.  It is considered as pavement preservation because thin layer does not add structural capacity.
